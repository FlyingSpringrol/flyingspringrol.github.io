About me

My meandering path

I attended UC San Diego right after high school, where I studied neuroscience and cell biology. Towards the end of my first year, I wrote a research grant to explore the biochemical mechanisms of dreaming... a grant which immediately got rejected. Based on this, and the growing realization that neuroscience had decades to go before it answered my questions about consciousness, thought, and dreaming, I decided to drop out of school (2014-2015). In my gap year, I worked at Pottery Barn Kids, lived at my then-girlfriends mom's house, and taught myself to code during the nights (2015-2016). After a year of this, I moved to Berkeley, went to school at Berkeley city college, and started a company. (PantheonVR). We ended up getting into YCombinator, which I did for a couple months until I realized consumer VR was too early. (2016-2017). I decided to leave the company and take my acceptance as a junior transfer to UC Berkeley, where I'm currently finishing my degree in computer science. I'm set to graduate at the end of Fall 2018.

Because of on my background in neuroscience, I'm incredibly interested in human-centered design and creating things that interact intuitively and naturally with the human brain. I'm also particularly interested in VR/AR, based on that fact that a) it's so frickin cool and b) it's novel and fun to design for. I also think VR will absolutely change the way people learn in the future: the similarity to how experts can visualize and interact with their mental models is far too similar to how anyone can interact with models in VR. My interest in AR kind of grew naturally out of my development work with VR, mobileAR is essentially frictionless compared to the nightmare an Oculus is to set up.

I'm also extremely passionate about where technology intersects with education. I think education is the key to a world where people can understand and empathize with each other, and I think technology is a key to getting to that point. I'd be excited to work with any company or research group that's looking to work on this problem.

PantheonVR (2016-2017 work)

Pantheon was a 3D modeling tool for VR. Essentially the problem we were trying to solve was the difficulty of content creation for VR. A content creator has to understand the full pipeline to make anything: 3D modeling, texturing, and rendering. This entails understanding Photoshop, Quixel/Substance Painter, Maya/Blender, and Unity. We tried to compress everything into one application so someone would be able to create in VR. We ended up doing YCombinator for Winter 2017, but I left the company when I realized consumer VR idea was not ready.
Link

Memorease (2016 work)

Memorease was designed to be a VR tool for learning. It was my first deep dive into product design and VR development. I learned Blender, Unity, Quixel, and photoshop to texture and 3D model everything. Eventually we realized it would take far to long for us to make everything in the human body, so we pivoted to start development on Pantheon.
